The Scottish Crossbill (Loxia scotica) is a small passerine bird in the finch family Fringillidae. It is endemic to the Caledonian Forests of Scotland, and is the only bird (and vertebrate) unique to the United Kingdom.[1][2][3] The Scottish Crossbill was confirmed as a unique species in August 2006, on the basis of having a distinctive bird song.[4][5][6]
